A Chinese family is expected to welcome
grandparents into the home in order to
take care of them in their old age.

When this happened in my home,
Chinglish was replaced with Chinese and
I started inviting my friends over less and less.
I eliminated the possibility for my friends
to make fun of their accents, of our
"strange sounding" language.
Or at least that was how I justified it.

BUT WHO WAS I REALLY
PROTECTING: MY FAMILY
OR MY EGO?

Language is a part of my culture.

To my grandparents, you have a weird accent too.

I HAVE GROWN TO HAVE NOTHING BUT APPRECIATION AND PRIDE FOR THE LANGUAGE OF MY FAMILY.

Our language is what connects my family. My tongue follows the same patterns as those of my ancestors. My hand mimics the strokes of their calligraphy. I am connected to not only my family, but a large community of people who not only look like me, but carry similar values as well. How could I ever be ashamed of such a thing?
